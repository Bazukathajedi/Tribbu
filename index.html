Dr Oliver "Tuku" Mtukudzi

The greatest Afro-Jazz legend

The Life and Death of Tuku:

    Oliver "Tuku" Mtukudzi well known as Tuku was born on the 22nd of September 1952 in Highfield, Harare, Zimbabwe and died peacefully at Avenues Clinic in Harare on the 23rd of January 2019 aged 66. He survived with five children, two grandchildren and married to Daisy Mtukudzi.

    During his life's work, the legendary Afro-Jazz musician has 67 Albums under his belt. Tuku released his first album in 1978 called "Ndipeiwo Zano" and his 67th album called "Hanya'ga(concern)" in 2018. Mtukudzi had 24 musical awards to his name and also starred in 5 films naming Neria(1993) among others, which he also produced the soundtrack.

    Apart from being a musician, Mtukudzi was a businessman, philanthropist, human rights activist and was named UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador for Southern African Region up untill the time of his death. Mtukudzi also built an arts center in 2004 named Pakare Paye Arts Center. This establishment the legend left focuses on talent nurturing, art s and career development for the future musical and artistic generations to follow.
